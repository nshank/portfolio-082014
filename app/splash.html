SplashShopE-Commerce Website

Interaction Design | Research | Content Strategy

SplashShop is a marketplace run by the Chicago Sun Times Splash publication to showcase products from local Chicago businesses. It uses a third-party platform to manage the multivendor shops as well as the transaction process.

Process

As a member of the design team that was asked to evaluate and redesign the presentation layer to meet Splash requirements, I focused on the product pages and the checkout process for both desktop and mobile. I started by doing a competitive analysis to find best practices on e-commerce experiences. I then wireframed the mobile experience for the SplashShop. This included layout of how the products were presented as well as the check out/purchase process and how it could be most efficient on a mobile phone. Wireframes and visual designs were handed off to a front-end developer within our team for implementation. This process included multiple iteraions of the interations, visual design and front-end developmet.

Role

The launching of SplashShop was a true team effort. I worked with a visual designer, a front-end developer, and a business team that consisted of internal as well as external people. I was also tasked with managing the relationship between our team and the third party vendor.

Outcomes

SplashShop launched in November of 2013.
